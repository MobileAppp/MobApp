Circumference of a circle

Perimeter of a circle
The perimeter of a shape is the distance around the out side of the shape. The "circumference" of a circle is the distance around the circle. The distance across a circle is called the "diameter". "Radius" of a circle is the distance from the centre to the circumference.

FORMULAR: Circumference = 2πr

where

π = 3.14

r = radius of the circle

OR
Circumference = πd

where

π = 3.14

d = diameter of the circle

ILLUSTRATION:
;

EXAMPLE 1:
Calculate the circumference of a circle with radius 5m

Solution

Circumference = 2πr
Circumference = 2 x 3.14 x 5
Circumference = 31.4m

EXAMPLE 2:
Calculate the circumference of a circle with diameter 8cm

Solution

Circumference = πr
Circumference = 3.14 x 8cm
Circumference = 25.12cm
